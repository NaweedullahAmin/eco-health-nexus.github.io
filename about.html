Principal Investigator

Leadership and expertise driving our research initiatives

Naweedullah Amin

Principal Investigator
Department of Zoology, Faculty of Biology
Kabul University, Afghanistan

Naweedullah Amin is a faculty member in the Department of Zoology, Faculty of Biology, Kabul University. He has over six years of academic and research experience in aquatic ecotoxicology, water quality assessment, and environmental pollution studies. His work focuses on the impacts of chemical pollutants and nanoparticles on aquatic organisms, with an emphasis on fish physiology, ecosystem health, and environmental risk assessment.

Mr. Amin holds a Master's degree in Ecology from Universiti Putra Malaysia, where he graduated with a perfect GPA (4.00) under the competitive World Bank HEDP Scholarship. He earned his Bachelor's degree in Biology with distinction from Kabul University. His research findings have been published in several reputable international journals, and he has presented at recognized conferences, including the International Fundamental Science Congress in Malaysia.

Research Expertise & Technical Skills

  • Aquatic Ecotoxicology
  • Water Quality Assessment
  • Nanoparticle Toxicology
  • Environmental Risk Assessment
  • Fish Physiology & Ecosystem Health
  • Biostatistical Analysis (Python, SPSS)
  • Advanced Laboratory Techniques
  • XRD, DLS, TEM, ICP-MS Analysis

He currently serves as Head of the Zoology Department, while also contributing as a member of the Faculty Academic Council, University Research Council, and University Promotions Committee. His additional roles include leading the Faculty Research Committee and supervising undergraduate research projects.

Mr. Amin is also an active peer reviewer for international journals, including Ecotoxicology and Environmental Safety. His technical expertise includes biostatistical analysis (Python, SPSS), advanced laboratory techniques (XRD, DLS, TEM, ICP-MS), and interdisciplinary environmental research methodologies.

He is recognized for his contributions to advancing environmental science research in Afghanistan and for supporting the development of young researchers through academic mentorship and capacity-building activities.
