Our Mission

We are a research team based at Kabul University dedicated to understanding how environmental contaminants from nanoparticles to plastic waste impact aquatic ecosystems and public health in Afghanistan. Our work bridges laboratory science, field ecology, and social behavior surveys to generate solutions for a healthier environment.

Principal Investigator

Naweedullah Amin

Head of Zoology Department | Ecotoxicology Researcher |Assistant Professor

Ecotoxicology researcher and university lecturer with over 6 years of academic experience in assessing the environmental impacts of pollutants on aquatic species' behavior, physiology, and ecosystems.

Research Focus

  • Nanoparticle Ecotoxicology: Investigating developmental toxicity of metal oxide nanoparticles in aquatic models like Javanese Medaka.
  • Water Quality & Fish Health: Assessing aquaculture conditions and fish health using biometric indices and water quality parameters.
  • Plastic Pollution & Public Behavior: Applying the Theory of Planned Behavior to understand and mitigate plastic waste in Kabul.
  • Environmental Public Health: Studying knowledge, attitudes, and practices regarding water safety, air quality, and fish consumption.
